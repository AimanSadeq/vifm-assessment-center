# VIFM-AC Competency Framework — Research Grounding & Citations

**Companion to:** `competency-framework-v2-design.md` ·
**Purpose:** Anchor every competency in **peer-reviewed research**, with
citations. This is what lets VIFM state — accurately — that the framework and its
assessment questions are *grounded in peer-reviewed research*, and it
simultaneously establishes **original, clean-room authorship** (definitions
derived from the published literature, not from any commercial framework's text).

> **What this establishes (and what it doesn't).**
> Grounding in the literature gives the framework **content and construct
> validity by design** — defensible to clients today. It does **not** by itself
> establish **criterion/predictive validity** (that scores predict performance in
> *your* workforce); that is an optional, later empirical study, only if a client
> requires it. State the claim precisely: *"derived from and consistent with
> peer-reviewed research,"* not *"empirically proven to predict performance."*
>
> **Citation status:** all sources below are real, established works, attributed
> to the correct authors/journals. Volume/page details should get one final
> reference-manager pass before client publication.

---

## Cross-cutting foundations

These works underpin the whole model and the competency-based assessment method:

- **Competency taxonomy & validation:** Bartram (2005) "Great Eight"
  competencies; Tett, Guterman, Bleier & Murphy (2000) hyperdimensional
  taxonomy of managerial competence.
- **Performance theory:** Campbell, McCloy, Oppler & Sager (1993) *A theory of
  performance*.
- **Domains of managerial competence:** Hogan & Warrenfeltz (2003) — intrapersonal,
  interpersonal, leadership, business skills (maps to our SELF / PEOPLE /
  RESULTS / THINKING domains).
- **Trait → performance base rates:** Barrick & Mount (1991) Big Five
  meta-analysis.

## THINKING

### Cluster 1 — Strategic & Commercial Reasoning
1. **Forward Strategy Setting** — strategic thinking as a learnable, synthetic,
   future-oriented capability. *(Liedtka, 1998; Mintzberg, 1994.)*
2. **Commercial & Market Awareness** — practical intelligence / tacit knowledge
   applied to the business context. *(Wagner & Sternberg, 1985.)*
3. **Financial Literacy & Acumen** — financial literacy as a measurable
   competence affecting decision quality. *(Lusardi & Mitchell, 2014.)*
4. **Critical Analysis** — critical-thinking constructs and transfer.
   *(Facione, 1990; Halpern, 1998.)*
5. **Sound Judgement** — judgment/decision-making under uncertainty; when
   expertise/intuition is and isn't reliable. *(Kahneman & Klein, 2009.)*

### Cluster 2 — Innovation & Complexity
6. **Creative Problem-Solving** — componential model of organizational
   creativity; innovative work behaviour. *(Amabile, 1988; Scott & Bruce, 1994.)*
7. **Navigating Complexity** — cognitive/behavioural complexity in managers.
   *(Streufert & Swezey, 1986.)*
8. **Systems & Global Perspective** — cultural intelligence; cross-boundary
   judgment. *(Earley & Ang, 2003; Ang et al., 2007.)*
9. **Digital & Data Fluency** — digital capability in the future workforce.
   *(Colbert, Yee & George, 2016.)*

## RESULTS

### Cluster 3 — Delivery & Execution
10. **Proactive Initiative** — proactive behaviour / personal initiative.
    *(Bateman & Crant, 1993; Frese & Fay, 2001.)*
11. **Outcome Ownership** — goal-setting theory and goal-directed performance.
    *(Locke & Latham, 2002.)*
12. **Accountability for Commitments** — psychology of accountability and its
    effects on effort and judgment. *(Lerner & Tetlock, 1999.)*
13. **Planning & Prioritisation** — goal/plan structuring (Locke & Latham, 2002)
    and conscientiousness as a performance predictor. *(Barrick & Mount, 1991.)*
14. **Process Optimisation** — quality-management theory underlying continuous
    improvement. *(Anderson, Rungtusanatham & Schroeder, 1994.)*

### Cluster 4 — Adaptability & Change
15. **Operating Through Uncertainty** — adaptive performance taxonomy; tolerance
    of ambiguity. *(Pulakos et al., 2000; Furnham & Ribchester, 1995.)*
16. **Learning by Doing** — experiential learning; learning agility.
    *(Kolb, 1984; DeRue, Ashford & Myers, 2012.)*
17. **Resilience Under Pressure** — psychological resilience; psychological
    capital. *(Bonanno, 2004; Luthans et al., 2007.)*
18. **Mobilising Around Purpose** — transformational / charismatic leadership
    (vision articulation). *(Bass, 1985; Conger & Kanungo, 1987.)*

## PEOPLE

### Cluster 5 — Influence & Communication
19. **Clear & Adaptive Communication** — interpersonal communication competence.
    *(Spitzberg & Cupach, 1984.)*
20. **Persuasion & Buy-in** — influence tactics; science of social influence.
    *(Yukl & Falbe, 1990; Cialdini & Goldstein, 2004.)*
21. **Constructive Conflict Handling** — task vs. relationship conflict and
    outcomes (meta-analysis). *(De Dreu & Weingart, 2003.)*
22. **Principled Negotiation** — negotiation research synthesis.
    *(Thompson, Wang & Gunia, 2010.)*
23. **Relationship Networks** — social capital / structural holes; network
    position and outcomes. *(Burt, 1992; Ibarra, 1993.)*

### Cluster 6 — Leading & Developing Others
24. **Coaching & Talent Growth** — managerial coaching behaviour and outcomes.
    *(Ellinger, Ellinger & Keller, 2003.)*
25. **Building Cohesive Teams** — the "Big Five" of teamwork.
    *(Salas, Sims & Burke, 2005.)*
26. **Cross-Functional Collaboration** — task interdependence and group
    effectiveness. *(Wageman, 1995.)*
27. **Trust & Credibility** — integrative model of organizational trust
    (ability, benevolence, integrity). *(Mayer, Davis & Schoorman, 1995.)*
28. **Interpersonal Adaptability** — self-monitoring and behavioural flexibility.
    *(Snyder, 1974.)*

## SELF

### Cluster 7 — Integrity & Character
29. **Self-Insight** — managerial self-awareness and self–other rating
    agreement. *(Church, 1997.)*
30. **Emotional Regulation & Empathy** — emotional intelligence as an ability.
    *(Mayer, Salovey & Caruso, 2008.)*
31. **Principled Courage** — employee voice / speaking up.
    *(Morrison, 2011.)*
32. **Ethical Conduct** — ethical leadership; integrity-test validity.
    *(Brown, Treviño & Harrison, 2005; Ones, Viswesvaran & Schmidt, 1993.)*
33. **Cultural & Inclusive Sensitivity** — cultural intelligence.
    *(Ang et al., 2007.)*

### Cluster 8 — Growth & Personal Effectiveness
34. **Adaptive Learning Capacity** — learning agility, conceptual grounding.
    *(DeRue, Ashford & Myers, 2012.)*
35. **Continuous Self-Development** — antecedents of voluntary development
    activity. *(Maurer & Tarulli, 1994.)*
36. **Composure Under Stress** — stress appraisal and coping; emotional
    stability. *(Lazarus & Folkman, 1984; Barrick & Mount, 1991.)*
37. **Sustainable Wellbeing** — job demands–resources model.
    *(Bakker & Demerouti, 2007.)*
38. **Resource Mobilisation** — managerial resourcefulness.
    *(Kanungo & Misra, 1992.)*

## Candidate GCC banking-specific additions (research-supported)

If you want sector-specific competencies (recommended for banking), these are
also literature-supported and can be added/validated:

  integrity literature above (Brown et al., 2005; Ones et al., 1993).
- **Risk & Control Vigilance (incl. AML/financial-crime)** — accountability and
  conscientiousness literatures (Lerner & Tetlock, 1999; Barrick & Mount, 1991).
