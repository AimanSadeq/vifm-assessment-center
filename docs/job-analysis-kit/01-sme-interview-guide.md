# 01 — SME Structured Interview Guide

**Duration:** 45–60 min · **Participant:** high-performing incumbent or their
supervisor · **Recorder:** interviewer captures notes on the role profile
(`05`).

---

## Pre-brief & consent (read aloud)

> "Thank you for taking part. We're building VIFM's own competency framework for
> [role family]. I'll ask about what the role involves and what separates strong
> performers from adequate ones. There are no right or wrong answers — your
> experience is the data. May I record this session? The recording is used only
> to build the framework, kept confidential, and anonymised. You can stop or skip
> any question at any time."

Confirm: ☐ consent to participate ☐ consent to record

---

## Section A — Role context (10 min)

1. In one or two sentences, what is the core **purpose** of this role — why does
   it exist?
2. What are the **3–6 key accountabilities** (the outcomes the role is
   responsible for)?
3. Who are the main **stakeholders** (internal and external/clients)?
4. How is success **measured** (KPIs, targets, quality/compliance standards)?

## Section B — Task & activity elicitation (10 min)

5. Walk me through a typical week. What does the person actually *do*?
6. Which tasks are **most critical** to getting results — where do mistakes hurt
   most (e.g., credit decisions, client handling, regulatory reporting)?
7. Which tasks are **hardest to do well**, and why?

## Section C — Performance differentiators *(the heart of it)* (15–20 min)

8. Think of an **outstanding** performer in this role. What do they *do* that
   sets them apart — specific behaviours, not traits?
9. Now think of someone who was **technically capable but ultimately
   ineffective**. What was missing — behaviourally?
10. When two people have the same technical knowledge, what makes one **far more
    effective** than the other?
11. What behaviours would make someone **fail** or be a **risk** in this role
    (e.g., cutting compliance corners, poor client confidentiality)?

*(Each answer here should yield candidate competencies — note them against the
draft list in `04`, and capture concrete examples as critical incidents in
`02`.)*

## Section D — Future orientation (5 min)

12. How is this role **changing** — regulation, digital/AI, client expectations,
    GCC market shifts?
13. What capabilities will matter **more in 2–3 years** than they do today?

## Section E — Draft framework check (5 min)

14. *(Show the 8 clusters from the v2 draft.)* Which of these are most central to
    this role? Anything important here that's **missing**?

---

## Interviewer notes

- Push for **behaviour, not adjectives**: "What did they *do*?" beats "they were
  proactive."
- Stay neutral; don't lead toward the draft competencies in Sections A–C.
- Flag any capability that doesn't map to the 38 draft items — these are
  candidate **additions** (§5 decision rules in the README).
