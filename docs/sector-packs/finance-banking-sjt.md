# Sector Pack — Finance & Banking (SJT variants)

**Context:** GCC banking & finance. **Use with:** the universal framework
(`competency-framework-v2-design.md`), CBI questions and self-report scales (which
are sector-neutral), in place of — or alongside — the **neutral SJT base** in
`competency-items-cluster1.md` / `competency-items-clusters2-8.md`.

Each item targets the same competency and preserves the same MOST/LEAST key logic
as the neutral base, so scoring stays comparable. Confirm keys with sector SMEs +
pilot stats before high-stakes use. Option order is randomised at delivery.

---

## Cluster 1 — Strategic & Commercial Reasoning

**1. Forward Strategy Setting.** You lead a retail banking unit. A fintech is
rapidly taking payment share among under-30 customers, and the regulator has
signalled open-banking rules within ~18 months; the board wants this quarter's
targets protected. MOST/LEAST:
- A. Keep the team focused on this quarter; revisit the fintech threat next year.
- B. Commission a scenario analysis of open-banking impact and shape a 2–3 year
  response while protecting near-term targets.
- C. Immediately cut current product investment to fund an untested digital wallet.
- D. Wait for competitors to move, then copy the winning approach.

**Key:** MOST = **B**; LEAST = **A**.

**2. Commercial & Market Awareness.** A long-standing corporate client in
construction requests expanded credit. Oil prices have fallen 20% over two
quarters and the client's main revenue is government infrastructure spending.
MOST/LEAST:
- A. Approve based on the long relationship and clean repayment history.
- B. Assess the client's exposure to government spending under the new oil-price
  environment before deciding.
- C. Decline outright to avoid all risk.
- D. Approve a smaller facility with covenants tied to confirmed government
  contracts.

**Key:** MOST = **B**; LEAST = **A** (D partial credit).

**3. Financial Literacy & Acumen.** *(objective, single best answer)* A bank
reports rising net interest income, but its CET1 ratio fell from 13% to 10.5% and
its NPL ratio rose from 2% to 4%. Most accurate interpretation?
- A. Profitability gains are sustainable; capital and asset quality are not a
  concern.
- B. Despite higher income, weakening capital adequacy and rising NPLs signal
  increasing risk that could constrain growth. ✅
- C. The falling CET1 ratio is irrelevant as long as income is growing.
- D. Rising NPLs indicate stronger lending volume and are a positive sign.

**Key:** **B**.

**4. Critical Analysis.** A report states "Branch X is our best performer — highest
deposit growth." Branch X opened 6 months ago in a new high-income district.
MOST/LEAST:
- A. Accept it and roll out Branch X's tactics across the network.
- B. Examine whether the growth is driven by location/market factors vs. branch
  practices before drawing lessons.
- C. Dismiss the report — new branches always grow fast.
- D. Rank all branches by deposit growth and reward the top quartile.

**Key:** MOST = **B**; LEAST = **A**.

**5. Sound Judgement.** A suspected fraud pattern appears in a major client's
transactions. Confirming takes ~48 hours; freezing now could damage a key
relationship; doing nothing risks a regulatory breach and loss. MOST/LEAST:
- A. Wait for full confirmation to avoid upsetting the client.
- B. Apply targeted holds/monitoring on the suspicious activity, expedite the
  investigation, and inform compliance.
- C. Freeze all of the client's accounts immediately.
- D. Escalate to compliance but take no interim action.

**Key:** MOST = **B**; LEAST = **A**.

## Cluster 2 — Innovation & Complexity

**6. Creative Problem-Solving.** Many applicants abandon your branch's
account-opening process midway; the standard fix is to add staff. MOST/LEAST:
- A. Add more staff to process applications faster.
- B. Map where applicants drop off and redesign those steps, piloting a simpler
  digital flow.
- C. Accept the abandonment as normal for the industry.
- D. Copy a competitor's process wholesale without testing fit.

**Key:** MOST = **B**; LEAST = **C**.

**7. Navigating Complexity.** A product launch depends on legal, IT, compliance,
and three vendors, with conflicting timelines and incomplete data. MOST/LEAST:
- A. Push every party to hit the original date regardless of dependencies.
- B. Map the dependencies, identify the critical path, and sequence decisions
  around it.
- C. Delay the whole launch until everything is fully certain.
- D. Focus only on the parts you control and ignore the rest.

**Key:** MOST = **B**; LEAST = **A**.

**8. Systems & Global Perspective.** A risk model that works at home is to be
applied to a cross-border GCC expansion. MOST/LEAST:
- A. Apply the model unchanged to save time.
- B. Assess how local regulation, currency, and customer behaviour differ, then
  adapt the model.
- C. Discard the model and rebuild from scratch unnecessarily.
- D. Apply it but adjust only the currency field.

**Key:** MOST = **B**; LEAST = **A**.

**9. Digital & Data Fluency.** A manager runs monthly sales reporting on manual
spreadsheets with frequent errors. MOST/LEAST:
- A. Keep the spreadsheets; the team is used to them.
- B. Introduce an automated dashboard from source systems, with checks, and train
  the team.
- C. Outsource all reporting to a vendor without reviewing the data needs.
- D. Add a second person to double-check the spreadsheets.

**Key:** MOST = **B**; LEAST = **A**.

## Cluster 3 — Delivery & Execution

**11. Outcome Ownership.** Mid-quarter, your team is behind a key target due to a
vendor delay. MOST/LEAST:
- A. Report the vendor as the reason and lower expectations.
- B. Re-plan with the team to recover what's achievable while escalating the vendor

- C. Push the team harder without changing the plan.
- D. Wait to see if the vendor recovers on its own.

**Key:** MOST = **B**; LEAST = **A**.

**12. Accountability for Commitments.** You realise you'll miss a deadline you
promised a colleague. MOST/LEAST:
- A. Say nothing and hope they don't notice.
- B. Tell them as soon as you know, with a revised date and a plan.
- C. Deliver late without comment.
- D. Blame competing priorities when asked.

**Key:** MOST = **B**; LEAST = **A**.

**13. Planning & Prioritisation.** You have five tasks due this week and can't do
them all well. MOST/LEAST:
- A. Start with the easiest to clear the list.
- B. Rank by impact and deadline, focus on the highest-value items, and renegotiate
  the rest.
- C. Attempt all five at once.
- D. Do them in the order they arrived.

**Key:** MOST = **B**; LEAST = **A**.

**14. Process Optimisation.** A reconciliation process takes three days with
repeated rework. MOST/LEAST:
- A. Accept it as the cost of accuracy.
- B. Find where the rework originates, fix the root cause, then standardise the

- D. Add overtime to finish faster.

## Cluster 4 — Adaptability & Change

**15. Operating Through Uncertainty.** A regulatory change is announced, but final

- C. Guess at the final rules and advise clients firmly.

**16. Learning by Doing.** You're assigned a product area you've never worked in.
MOST/LEAST:

- C. Act as if you already know it to appear competent.
- D. Hand the work to someone more experienced.

**17. Resilience Under Pressure.** A major deal you led collapses publicly.

- C. Blame others to protect your standing.
- D. Carry on as if nothing happened, ignoring the lessons.

**18. Mobilising Around Purpose.** Your team is demoralised during a

## Cluster 5 — Influence & Communication

**19. Clear & Adaptive Communication.** You must explain a complex risk exposure to

**20. Persuasion & Buy-in.** Another department resists adopting a new shared

**21. Constructive Conflict Handling.** Two of your team members are in open

- C. Side with the more senior one to end it.
- D. Separate their work permanently to avoid contact.

**22. Principled Negotiation.** A key client demands a price you can't profitably

- D. Agree now and try to claw it back later.

**23. Relationship Networks.** You interact only with your immediate team and now

**33. Cultural & Inclusive Sensitivity.** A team member's cultural practice affects
